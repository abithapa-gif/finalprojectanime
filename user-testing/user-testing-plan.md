# User Testing Plan — Anime Vault

## Who I'll Test With

1. **Tester A** — A friend who watches anime casually (seen Demon Slayer and One Piece). Moderate tech comfort, uses their phone primarily. Represents the casual fan.
2. **Tester B** — A classmate who doesn't watch anime at all. Low familiarity with the content. Good for testing whether the site is navigable without prior knowledge.
3. **Tester C** — A fellow anime fan who watches all 5 series I cover. High tech comfort, also a web dev student. Will give both content and technical feedback.

---

## Tasks I'll Ask Them to Do

1. **Find information about Attack on Titan — how many episodes does it have and what studio made it.**
   Testing: Can they find the Series page? Is the info easy to locate within a card?

2. **Go to the contact page and send a message asking about fan theories.**
   Testing: Can they complete the form? Do the field labels make sense? Does the form feel trustworthy?

3. **Starting from the home page — find out who runs Anime Vault.**
   Testing: Can they find the About page and locate the team section?

---

## What I Want to Learn

- Is the 4-page navigation clear to someone unfamiliar with the site?
- Do the series cards on the Series page give enough info at a glance, or do users need to dig?
- Does the contact form feel like a legitimate form or a generic school project form?
- Is anything confusing on mobile specifically?
- Does the dark theme feel intentional or just dark-for-dark's-sake?
